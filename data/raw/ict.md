# Guidelines For Using ICT Boardroom/Classroom Technologies

## Classroom Technologies

### Technologies Available in Classrooms

With a total of 46 classes in the University, the ICT Services Team has engaged in installing various technologies to create a learning environment that is conducive for both Lecturers and Students. Below is a list of available technologies in all the classrooms:

#### Projector
The projectors are all networked to allow them to be powered over the network.

#### Desktop Computer
The PC is installed with standard software that is readily available for presentation by both Lecturers and Students.

#### Amplifier
The Amplifier is fine-tuned to be able to project surround sound to the room. It interconnects the speakers and the PC.

#### Speakers
Mounted speakers project surround sound in the classroom to allow for listening to audio and video clips used in teaching and learning.

#### VGA Switcher
The VGA Switcher is placed just below the Multimedia desk and is used to switch between different inputs. This box allows you to either use the PC in class or come with your laptop and use it in class for presentation.

#### Pop Box
The Pop Box allows for connectivity in that it allows you to come with your own device e.g. Laptop and connect to the projector. The box has VGA, HDMI, Audio and Power ports for ease of connecting your gadgets.

### Equipment Operation Instructions

#### DESKTOP COMPUTER/PC
- For use by Lecturers and/or students for projecting presentations
- Log off/Power off once done
- **Logon Credentials**:
  - Username = lecturer or .\lecturer (i.e. dot slash lecturer)
  - Password = inquire from faculty helpdesk (default for all Computers in the classes)

#### POWERING PROJECTOR
- Open Mozilla/Chrome browser, then click on the Projector Bookmark
- In the credential window that appears, use:
  - Username = EPSONREMOTE
  - Password = inquire from faculty helpdesk (default for all Projectors in the classes)
- Click on basic control then on the power button displayed on the Monitor
- Double click power button to switch off

#### AMPLIFIER
- For Surround Sound. It is connected to PC and Speakers in classes
- Can also be used with personal laptop
- Do not change its settings/alter the knobs – Control Volume from your computer
- Remember to Power off once done

#### POP BOX
- For enabling BYOD (Bring Your Own Device)
- It allows you to project or use sound from your laptop using either HDMI or VGA Connections
- It has the following sockets and ports: power, VGA, HDMI, Audio & LAN (Alternatively use Wi-Fi)
- Close it once done

#### SWITCHER
For switching modes between the Desktop PC and personal laptop in class. It is coded as below:
- A = Desktop PC (Default)
- B = Laptop VGA
- C = Laptop HDMI
- D = Blank
- Always Set it back to DEFAULT once done

#### REMOTE CONTROL
- Switch on the TV screen by pressing the RED power button on the REMOTE below the screen
- NB: Note the remote is placed directly below the TV screen
- The sources button is for switching display between the Desktop and the Laptop

#### TELEVISION SCREEN
- The TV screen is for projection
- The presentations (images) from the laptop or Desktop PC are projected on the TV
- NB: To navigate sources from the Desktop to the laptop use the sources button on the remote

#### HDMI CABLE
- HDMI cable provisions for connection between TV screen and the laptop/PC
- NB: Pick the end of the HDMI cable available and connect it to the HDMI port on your laptop

#### LAPTOP/PC
- The laptop/PC are for Input. Kindly make sure they are ON and logged in to display the output
- NB: PC is connected directly while the laptop must be connected by the user
- If connection is established but no display, PRESS Windows icon on the keyboard and P simultaneously. The window that appears, select duplicate

## ICT Regulations

These regulations are meant to serve two purposes: to regulate the use of the University's Information and Communication Technologies and Resources, and to educate users on their responsibilities in relation to the use of these resources.

### I. Introduction

#### 1. Definitions:
- **Information and Communication Technology Resources (ICT Resources)** include application systems, Information, ICT infrastructure and people owned by, or under the jurisdiction of, the University.
- **Applications Systems** are the automated user systems and manual procedures that process the information.
- **Information** is the data, in all their forms, input, processed and output by the information systems in whatever form is used by the business.
- **ICT Infrastructure** is the technology and facilities that enable the processing of the applications.
- **People** are the personnel required to plan, organize, acquire, implement, deliver, support, monitor and evaluate the information systems and services.
- **ICT resources' owners** are designated individuals under whose jurisdiction particular information system resources have been placed.
- **Authorization** is permission granted by the owner of an ICT resource to a user to use that resource.
- **Scope**: These regulations have the status of "University Regulations" which apply to all members of the University.
- **Director, Information and Communications Technology Services**: the person designated as the head of the University's ICT services.
- **ICT Resource Custodian**: Director of ICT Services is the Custodian of all shared ICT resources and services.
- **Acceptable use** refers to rules governing the good use of ICT resources in the University.
- **Unacceptable use** refers to bad conduct that users must avoid when using the University's ICT resources.

### II. Regulations

#### 1. Access Management
All users of the University's ICT resources must be authorized to access the appropriate systems and their resources. Access is controlled and monitored in accordance with University Statutes.

##### 1.1 Identification
- All system users are assigned unique IDs to use in accessing the University's systems and applications
- User IDs Must Not be shared
- Users are responsible for maintaining the security of their IDs and all activity occurring under those IDs
- In special circumstances, temporary generic accounts may be approved by the ICT Director

##### 1.2 Authorization
- Every user shall require authorization before using any ICT resources
- No one shall use any University ICT resource without proper authorization
- Users shall not disclose any information which may lead to compromise in the security of the University's ICT resources
- A user shall not misrepresent his or her identity or relationship to the University when obtaining or using University ICT resources

##### 1.3 Authentication
- Authentication ensures an identity
- Each ID requires a technique, usually a password, for validating identity
- Standards apply to all systems requiring authentication

##### 1.4 Account Management
- System custodians must be authorized by the system owner before giving users access to a system
- System Custodians must determine who should be able to get network access from off campus or remote locations
- A quarterly review of all system access levels of users should be carried out
- When employees terminate employment or change positions, Human Resource Office should advise ICT system custodians to modify access rights

##### 1.5 Privileged Users Access
- Certain system users have high-level access rights (System Administrators)
- Staff with high-level access rights should abide by a high Code of Ethics
- The ICT Department should constantly review access rights for System Administrators
- System Administrators found guilty of breaching security are subject to disciplinary action

##### 1.6 Contractors, Vendors & Third party access
- Access is permitted only if authorized by the Systems Owner and agreed by System Custodian
- These parties must comply with access control standards
- All temporary accounts should have an expiration date based on contract completion date

#### 2. Asset Security Management

##### 2.1 Server & System Backup
- All critical University information must be backed up on a regular basis
- Frequency of backup is determined by the frequency with which the data changes

##### 2.2 Personal Computer and Mobile Device Backup
- All critical University information should be stored on centrally maintained corporate networked disk storage
- Data stored on desktops, laptops and other mobile devices becomes the responsibility of the user to ensure it is backed up

##### 2.3 Recovery
- All backups of critical data must be tested periodically
- System Administrators must document all restore procedures
- Backup media must be retrievable within 24 hours, 365 days a year

##### 2.4 Off-Site Storage
- Off-site is synonymous with "out of the building"
- The off-site storage location must provide evidence of adequate fire and theft protection and environmental controls
- A formal Service Level Agreement (SLA) must exist with the off-site storage provider

##### 2.5 Data Retention
- Owners of University data are responsible for defining and documenting the length of time data must be retained
- The retention period, legal requirements, responsible parties, and source of legal requirement should be specified

##### 2.6 Business Continuity
- Business Continuity and Disaster Recovery Plans should be prepared and tested for all major systems
- A copy of each plan should be stored offsite
- A review of any major disruption to information services should be undertaken

##### 2.7 Security
All major information assets must be accounted for and have a nominated custodian. Different types of data require different levels of security:
- Public data can generally be made available to the general public
- Proprietary data is for internal University use and not for external distribution
- Restricted data is to be used only by individuals who require it in the course of performing their University responsibilities

#### 3. Acceptable Use of ICT Resources
The University's mission can be understood broadly as including education, research, self-training, and discussion on a wide range of subjects, not just those immediately necessary for a person's job or course of study.

- Users must comply with all applicable Acts of Parliament and jurisdiction by laws
- All users shall share ICT resources in accordance with policies set for the ICT resources
- Users must seek permission to read, alter, or delete another person's computer files or electronic mail
- Staff shall be permitted to use these Resources for outside consulting jobs provided the University is reimbursed
- Whilst the University network is being used to access outside networks, any abuses against such networks will be regarded as an unacceptable use
- Users are responsible for the security of their files in the University computers
- Users shall take full responsibility for information that they transmit through the University ICT Resources

#### 4. Unacceptable Use of ICT Resources
The University ICT Resources must not be used for the following activities:
- The creation, dissemination, storage and display of political campaigns, personal fund-raising, commercial enterprises, mass mailings
- The creation, dissemination, storage and display of obscene or pornographic material
- The downloading, storage and disseminating of copyrighted materials without permission
- Any activities which do not conform to the Kenyan Laws
- The deliberate interference with or gaining illegal access to user accounts and data
- Use of a username and password belonging to another user
- Attempts to falsify your identity
- Attempts to crack passwords or decode encrypted data
- Any other use that may bring the name of the University into disrepute
- Intentional creation, execution, forwarding or introduction of any viruses, worms, trojans
- Deliberate actions that might reduce the effectiveness of any antivirus or other ICT security controls
- Purposefully scanning internal or external machines
- Engaging in commercial activities that are not under the auspices of the University
- Using computing resources in such a way that it causes excessive strain on the computer systems
- Connecting any computing device to the University network unless it meets the security standards

#### 5. Administrative Computing Systems
- Administrative Computing systems deal with financial, academic, administrative, or other business information
- Every administrative computing system must have a designated Business Owner
- System owners have the responsibility to ensure that each system meets its functional requirements

#### 6. Publishing of web content
Users who publish World Wide Web pages or similar information on University ICT Resources shall take full responsibility for what they publish and shall respect the Acceptable-Use and Unacceptable-Use requirements.

#### 7. Internet Use
The University encourages the use of the Internet for the purpose of educational research and learning and to allow greater efficiency in teaching, research, administrative and service functions.

#### 8. Electronic Boards
Users shall comply with the regulations and policies of newsgroups, mailing lists, and other public forums through which they disseminate messages.

#### 9. Privacy Policy
The University fully supports and where possible observes the internationally recognized standards of personal data privacy protection, in compliance with the legal requirement of Personal Data (Privacy).

#### 10. Access to information after termination
A leaving employee may require certain information resources held, or services to be provided by the University. The following procedure will be followed:
- Request access through the Executive Director for Human Resources
- The Executive Director for HR determines if such access is allowable and requests ICTS to provide the resources
- A terminated employee shall not access information resources directly unless authorized in writing

#### 11. Penalties
Violation of these regulations shall constitute an offense and shall incur similar disciplinary measures as violations of other University regulations, including criminal prosecution in serious cases.

#### 12. Disclaimer
The University accepts no responsibility for the loss of any data or software or the failure of any security or privacy mechanism. No claim shall be made against the University or its employees in respect of any loss alleged to have been caused.
