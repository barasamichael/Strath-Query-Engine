The Bachelor of Business Information Technology course is offered in two stages: Stage I (Year I and II) and Stage II (Year III and IV). ### Master of Science in Spectrum Management

#### About Course
The Master's in Spectrum Management will offer students a solid system of training in the theory and practice of modern Spectrum Management (SM). Based on an overview of the current situation, the programme will create a unique and credible niche, complementing the existing professional Spectrum Management training options and promoting the harmonization of Spectrum Management practices.

To that end, the Masters in Spectrum Management is broad in scope and international in character, with tutors and participating institutions of the highest quality, so as to ensure the value of the programme and the reputation of its diploma. The programme strives to establish itself as the global "gold standard" for SM training.

An essential differentiator for the Master's in Spectrum Management would be formalised assessment of learning outcomes in terms of professional skills and qualifications obtained, supported by the award of a diploma representing a recognised academic level.

#### Programme Structure
Core modules can be reviewed as follows (because of new curriculum and emerging trends in the area):
- Spectrum Engineering
- Advanced Wireless Communication Systems
- Dynamic Spectrum Access and Cognitive Radio
- Spectrum Monitoring
- Economic and Market Tools of Spectrum Management

#### Course Requirement
To qualify for the award of the Masters in Spectrum Management, the student must take and pass all required modules of the programme.

#### Expected Learning Outcomes
At the end of the programme, the students will be able:
1. Apply spectrum management principles in a complex, multi-layered framework across international, regional, and national levels, demonstrated through real-world case studies and policy implementation exercises.
2. Use the SM concepts to assess the roles and functions of key stakeholders and the legal frameworks governing spectrum management, with an emphasis on producing stakeholder analysis reports and legal assessments.
3. Demonstrate proficiency in the operational principles and processes involved in authorizing radio services (licensing) through simulations and applied licensing exercises for radio services.
4. Integrate interdisciplinary expertise from technical, business, management, and policy aspects of spectrum management into a comprehensive final project, reflecting a holistic approach to managing spectrum challenges.
5. Contribute to research by producing a research proposal on a contemporary issue in spectrum management, positioning students for further studies (e.g., PhD) or professional research roles

### Master of Science in Sustainable Energy Transitions (MSc. SET)

#### About Course
Access to affordable and reliable energy is a requirement for socio-economic development. As such, universal energy access by the year 2030 has been prioritised globally through Sustainable Development Goal 7. To attain this goal, multilateral banks and development agencies such as The African Development Bank, World Bank and the United Nations have initiated various projects in developing countries with the aim of improving energy access by 2030.

These projects include Kenya Off-Grid Solar Access Project jointly implemented by the Kenyan Government and the World Bank and the Program for Infrastructure Development in Africa implemented by the African Union. Kenya has adopted this goal nationally through Vision 2030. Currently, Kenya reports that households particularly in rural areas are unable to afford the cost of grid connection despite heavy subsidies received through initiatives like the Last Mile Connectivity Project.

Further, despite numerous projects being implemented to promote a transition to clean cooking fuels and technologies, the Stockholm Environment Institute indicates that 67,000 people in Africa perish annually because of respiratory illnesses associated with indoor air pollution from 'dirty' fuels such as charcoal and firewood.

Multi-disciplinary education is therefore an opportunity to equip individuals with capacity to solve such complex problems. Strathmore University therefore introduces the MSc. in Sustainable Energy Transitions to equip students with key technical skills required to develop and implement energy systems as well as a clear understanding of the ecosystem in which they operate and understanding of key concepts from other academic disciplines which are important for the energy sector. Graduates from this program will be able to utilise their skills and collaborate with other professionals from different academic backgrounds to address complex societal challenges.

#### Why the MSc. SET Programme?
1. MSc SET is the first multi-disciplinary program in the energy sector that seeks to equip students with technical skills such as energy systems design and implementation, research and data analytics, energy systems auditing, entrepreneurship and innovation, decentralization and digitalization of the energy systems, as well as the necessary skills in policy, project management, economics, ethics, communication and others that will contribute to their development as well-rounded professionals.

2. This program will also provide industry exposure through the internship and capstone project thereby exposing students to practical challenges and solutions existing in the sector.

3. This program will provide a solution for students seeking a program with strong multi-disciplinary elements by offering local presence and part-time nature thereby allowing students to work, reside in the country and pursue their Masters degrees without incurring additional costs and loosing preestablished networks.

4. The programme will also provide international exposure by allowing students to receive guest lectures from renown academics. Some of these academics are currently partners of the Strathmore Energy Research Centre who are working on research projects locally. Students may also therefore take advantage of the opportunity to undertake their Masters thesis from research projects jointly being undertaken by SERC and international partners.

#### Who Should Attend?
This program should be attended by individuals seeking to work towards the development of the energy sector and implementation of relevant policies, technology systems and entrepreneurial solutions.

The course has been designed for students who have completed a degree in Engineering, Computer Science, Information Technology, Environmental Science, or a science related discipline from recognized universities.

#### Research Focus Areas

**Critical Infrastructure Protection**

Critical Infrastructure Protection involves safeguarding essential systems and services that are vital to national security, economic stability, and public safety. This includes the protection of Industrial Control Systems (ICS) and Supervisory Control and Data Acquisition (SCADA) networks, which are critical for managing and monitoring industrial processes. It also entails securing smart grids and renewable energy systems against cyber-physical attacks that could disrupt energy distribution and supply. Furthermore, effective protection strategies encompass the development and application of risk assessment models for public transport systems and autonomous vehicles, ensuring resilience against emerging threats and vulnerabilities.

**Cybersecurity in Emerging Technologies**

Cybersecurity in Emerging Technologies focuses on developing and applying advanced security measures to protect next-generation digital systems. This includes establishing robust security frameworks for Internet of Things (IoT) ecosystems in smart cities, ensuring that interconnected devices and services remain resilient against cyber threats. It also involves leveraging blockchain technology for secure data sharing and supply chain verification, enhancing transparency, trust, and integrity across distributed networks. Additionally, artificial intelligence and machine learning models are employed for predictive threat detection, enabling proactive identification and mitigation of potential cyberattacks before they cause significant harm.

**Zero Trust Architectures**

Zero Trust Architectures emphasize a security model where no user, device, or application is inherently trusted, regardless of whether they operate inside or outside the network perimeter. This approach incorporates contextual access control tailored for dynamic and high-risk environments, ensuring that access decisions are based on real-time context such as user identity, device health, and activity patterns. It also employs micro-segmentation to isolate workloads and limit lateral movement within distributed systems, coupled with continuous authentication to verify user and device trustworthiness throughout each session, thereby minimizing the attack surface and enhancing overall resilience.

**Incident Response and Threat Intelligence**

Incident Response and Threat Intelligence focuses on strengthening an organization's ability to detect, analyze, and respond to cyber threats effectively. This includes the development of real-time cyber threat intelligence platforms that provide actionable insights for proactive defense. It also involves training on Security Operations Center (SOC) best practices to enhance monitoring, incident handling, and coordinated response capabilities. Additionally, digital forensics and malware analysis play a crucial role in investigating infrastructure incidents, enabling the identification of attack vectors, mitigation of ongoing threats, and the development of preventive measures to protect against future compromises.

The vision is clear: to remain at the forefront of cybersecurity innovation, not just responding to threats but anticipating them, and to continue building the skills, tools, and collaborations that will keep people, industries, and nations safe in an increasingly connected future.

#### Conclusion
The CSI Research Hub is more than an academic initiative; it is a strategic driver of Kenya's and Africa's digital resilience in an era where cybersecurity has become inseparable from national security, economic stability, and societal well-being. By combining rigorous research, hands-on training, and forward-looking innovation, the hub serves as both a think tank and a practical solutions center for addressing the complex cyber challenges facing governments, industries, and communities. Its work in capacity building ensures that the next generation of cybersecurity professionals is not only technically skilled but also equipped with the strategic mindset needed to anticipate, mitigate, and respond to evolving threats.

Through strong partnerships with government agencies, critical infrastructure operators, industry leaders, and international institutions, the CSI Hub extends its influence far beyond the university, shaping policy, guiding best practices, and setting new benchmarks for cybersecurity excellence in Africa. These collaborations amplify its ability to create real-world impact—whether through vulnerability assessments that directly strengthen national systems, or the development of cutting-edge security tools and frameworks that are ready for deployment in high-stakes environment

As digital transformation accelerates across the continent, the CSI Research Hub stands as a beacon of trust, innovation, and resilience. It embodies Strathmore University's commitment to thought leadership, regional collaboration, and the creation of sustainable, secure digital ecosystems. With its blend of academic rigor, industry engagement, and community impact, the hub is not just preparing for the future of cybersecurity, it is actively shaping it.

This page seeks to guide you through the application process to ensure you do not find it a daunting, overwhelming task. It assists a great deal to read brochures of programmes of interest thoroughly. A personalised admission process makes a great start for your stay at Strathmore University.

### The Application Process
1. **Choose a Course**
   Start your journey at Strathmore University by selecting a program that matches your passion and career goals. From cutting-edge technology to innovative engineering, our courses prepare you to excel in a dynamic world. Take the first step toward your future today!

2. **Confirmation of Application**
   Verify and finalize your application to ensure all details are accurate and complete. This step secures your spot and moves you closer to joining the School of Computing and Engineering Sciences at Strathmore University.

3. **Application Review**
   Your application is carefully assessed to ensure it meets the requirements for admission to the School of Computing and Engineering Sciences. This step brings you closer to joining Strathmore University and pursuing your academic goals.

4. **Application Interview**
   Engage in a one-on-one interview to showcase your passion, qualifications, and readiness to join the School of Computing and Engineering Sciences at Strathmore University. This is your opportunity to stand out and take a step closer to admission.

5. **Payment of Fees**
   Complete your fee payment to secure your admission and officially join the School of Computing and Engineering Sciences at Strathmore University. This step confirms your place and sets you on the path to academic success.
