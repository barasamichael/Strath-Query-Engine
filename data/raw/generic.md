# Strathmore University Governance and Information

## Governance/Administration

Strathmore University is a fully fledged university with a Vice Chancellor, three Deputy Vice Chancellors, the University Secretary, a number of senior management staff, the University Council, the Management Board, the Academic Council, and various committees.

### Legal Status
On April 23rd, 2008, His Excellency Mwai Kibaki, President of the Republic of Kenya granted a Charter to Strathmore University. The Charter provides for the establishment, control, governance, and administration of the university and for connected purposes, in the manner prescribed by the Universities Act (Chapter 210b, Laws of Kenya).

Strathmore University is sponsored by Strathmore Educational Registered Trustees (SERT), a non-profit body corporate with perpetual succession, established under the Trustee (Perpetual Succession) Act (Chapter 164 of the Revised Laws of Kenya 1962 edition).

Opus Dei is a Personal Prelature of the Catholic Church erected by Pope St. John Paul II on 28th November 1982. Through its activities, it seeks to spread the message that work, study, sport family life, and other ordinary activities are occasions for spiritual union with Jesus Christ.

Corporate apostolic initiatives promoted by Opus Dei, such as Strathmore University, are characterized by the presence of some faithful of Opus Dei, who together with ordinary citizens, undertake secular, lay activities, in accordance with the law of the land, based on professional work, and always imbued with a Christian spirit.

## About Us

Strathmore College was started in 1961 as an Advanced-level Sixth Form College offering Science and Arts subjects by a group of professionals, who formed a charitable Educational Trust (now the Strathmore Educational Trust). Strathmore College was the first in Pre-independence Kenya to accommodate all races, religions, and social standing. Saint Josemaría Escrivá, the founder of Opus Dei, inspired and encouraged them to start the College.

In March 1966, the first intake of Accountancy students, twenty-five in number, joined the Sixth Form students and began preparing for the examinations of the UK-based Association of Chartered Certified Accountants (ACCA). These first Accountancy students were sponsored by Shell East Africa, BAT (East Africa), and the East African Breweries. At this time Strathmore College was unique as a fully integrated post-Form 4 institution offering both academic and professional courses.

In October 1982, owing to the increased demand from companies for the professional training of their employees, the College began evening courses in Accountancy after normal working hours, with 60 students sponsored by various companies.

In 1986, in response to a request by the Trustees, the Government of Kenya donated 5 acres of land on Ole Sangale Road, Madaraka Estate. The European Union (EU) and the Italian Government agreed to back the Madaraka Campus project. The donors were keen to support a co-educational College that would offer courses in Management and Accountancy. Kianda College, an undertaking of Kianda Foundation, which was planning new developments at the time, agreed to run their professional courses in the new Madaraka campus.

Construction of the new campus commenced in September 1989. Meanwhile, in January 1991, the Information Technology Centre was started in the Lavington Campus to run computer courses leading to the Institute for the Management of Information Systems (formerly Institute of Data Processing Management) Diploma and Higher Diploma. In January 1992 a Distance Learning Centre was opened to offer correspondence courses in Accountancy to students who are unable to attend lectures.

In January 1993 Strathmore College merged with Kianda College and moved to Ole Sangale Road, Madaraka Estate.

With a total student body of 8189, comprising both part-time and full-time learners, our university prides itself in offering comprehensive educational opportunities to individuals from all walks of life. Our student community is richly diverse, with 86% of our students enrolled on a full-time basis and 14% pursuing their studies part-time. Embracing global perspectives, we welcome students from across the world, with 11% of our student population representing international communities.

### Key Milestones

In August 2002 the Commission of Higher Education awarded Strathmore a Letter of Interim Authority to operate as a University with a Faculty of Commerce and a Faculty of Information Technology.

The first undergraduate students to enroll in these faculties completed their 4-year degree course in December 2004 and graduated in August 2005.

In June 2007, Kenya's Commission for Higher Education approved the award of a charter to Strathmore University.

The University's Charter was gazetted under the Universities Act (CAP 210B) in the 'Kenya Gazette' supplement no. 47 (legislative supplement no. 27) via legal notice no. 86. The charter details the establishment and functions of the University, membership, and governance of the University, administration, financial provision, and statutes of the University.

### Timeline

- **1961**: Strathmore College launches an Advanced-level Sixth Form College (men only) offering Science and Arts tuition under the inspiration and encouragement of the founder of Opus Dei, St. Josemaria Escriva. Strathmore was Kenya's first multiracial multi-religious school.

- **1966**: The first intake of Accountancy students, 25 in number, joined the Sixth Form students and began preparing for examinations with the UK-based Association of Chartered Certified Accountants (ACCA).

- **1982**: Evening courses for Accountancy begin.

- **1989**: Construction of the new campus at Madaraka Estate, Ole Sangale Road commences in September.

- **1993**: Strathmore College merges with Kianda College (an institution founded in 1961 for women) and moves to the new Madaraka Campus.

- **2002**: The Commission for Higher Education (now Commission for University Education) awarded Strathmore a Letter of Interim Authority to operate a university and offer the flagship undergraduate programmes in Commerce and Information Technology.

- **2005**: Strathmore launches Strathmore Business School (SBS), as a graduate school to fill in the existing gap in leadership & management in Executive Education and MBA.

- **2007**: Strathmore is awarded a Charter by the Government of Kenya giving it full legal recognition to operate as a University.

- **2009**: Strathmore launches the School of Finance & Applied Economics (SFAE) to expand the programme offering from the initial focus on business & information technology.

- **2012**: Strathmore launches the School of Law (SLS) to further expand the academic offering and contribute to legal education and academic research in the country.

- **2015**: Strathmore launches a new 10-year strategic plan that provides vision and map for the university for the next 10 years. The mid-term review of the strategy was relaunched in November 2020.

- **2016**: @iLabAfrica is launched to spearhead ICT Research, Innovation, and Entrepreneurship with a mission of providing an environment that promotes technology innovation and business support structure and policy. The centre also houses @iBizAfrica, a business incubator for young entrepreneurs.

- **2019**: School of Management and Commerce (SMC) merges with the Business School to form the Strathmore University Business School (SBS).

- **2020**: The University launched the Institute of Management and Technology (formerly School of Accountancy) to re-align with TVET Act 2013.

- **2021**: Strathmore University Foundation Launch.

- **2022**: Faculty of Information Technology merges with the new engineering school to form the School of Computing and Engineering Sciences (SCES).

Lagos Business School, a key partner of Strathmore Business School received double accreditation from the Advanced Collegiate Schools of Business (AACSB) and the International Accreditation Advisory Board of the Association of MBAs (AMBA). AACSB is the leading global accreditation body for business schools offering undergraduate, master's, and doctorate degrees in business and accounting. ### Association of African Universities (AAU)
The Association of African Universities (AAU) is a pan-African organization that brings together universities and other institutions of higher education in Africa. It was founded in 1967 with the aim of promoting cooperation and collaboration among African universities, as well as to improve the quality of higher education in Africa.

### Association of Commonwealth Universities (ACU)
The Association of Commonwealth Universities (ACU) is an international organization that brings together universities and other institutions of higher education in the Commonwealth of Nations. It was founded in 1913, and is headquartered in London, England. The ACU has over 500 member institutions in 50 countries across the Commonwealth. These institutions represent a wide range of disciplines, and together they educate over 10 million students.

### International Association of Universities (IAU)
The International Association of Universities (IAU) is a membership-led non-governmental organization working in the field of higher education. It comprises more than 600 higher education institutions and organizations in over 130 countries. IAU is an official partner of UNESCO. The IAU secretariat is based in Paris and is located at the headquarters of UNESCO.

### Annual Investment Meeting (AIM Congress)
The Annual Investment Meeting (AIM Congress) is an Initiative of the AIM Global Foundation, an Independent International Organization fully committed to empower the World's Economy by boosting effective Promotion Strategies and facilitating Opportunities for Economic Productivity and Expansion.

## Coat of Arms, Mission, Vision & Values

### Motto
**Ut omnes unum sint** (That all may be one)

### Symbols

**The Three Hearts** represent the three races which, in 1961 when the University started, were segregated in the colonial system of education. The heart represents the person since it is taken as the source of all our actions, and the source of love. The fact that the three hearts all have the same color shows the equality of all people and their aim to love and understand each other. At the beginning, it clearly pointed at the target of racial unity. Today it symbolizes the common aim of parents, teachers, and students in the educational process of Strathmore.

**The Rose** in full bloom represents love, the source of all good desires and actions, even if at times this means loving sacrifice, as represented by the thorns. The rose has a supernatural meaning too. Love, with capital letters, is the love of God. The rose also has a historical meaning associated with the life of Saint Josemaría Escrivá, founder of Opus Dei, who inspired and encouraged the people who started Strathmore: he once received a divine sign in the form of a carved gilded rose in a very trying moment of his life.

**The Lion** is the symbol of strength and courage and of the determined fight for excellence and justice. It also represents Kenya, the country Strathmore was founded in, which like many African countries strives boldly to attain all the qualities mentioned above.

### Colors and Their Meanings

- **Blue (Azure)**: Sky blue means high ideals, high aims.
- **Red (Gules)**: Blood red means sacrifice, love, fortitude.

## Key Strategic Pillars

### Transformative Student Experience
Strathmore University continues to provide students with a continuum of diverse opportunities for intellectual, spiritual, and personal development extending well beyond the classroom to ensure that students achieve the maximum benefit of a Strathmore University education. We endeavor to broaden our students' horizons and attitudes while developing their current skills and abilities and learning new ones to help both their future careers and their role within society.

### Research and Innovation
Strathmore University engages in research and innovation that has an impact on learning, enterprise and society. Our main objective is to develop an open research culture and remove barriers to innovation for all our staff and students. Through this, we aim to be the partner of choice for all institutions seeking to do research and innovate in Africa.

### Service to Society
Our key goal in this pillar is to engage staff, students, alumni, and stakeholders in addressing ethical and societal challenges which contribute to SDG 1, which is to eradicate poverty. We aim to become instruments of societal thought and ethical leadership by having our students, staff, and alumni take a leading role as positive, transformative change agents in society. Strathmore University also has a dedicated team that coordinates the university's ecosystem to engage with the less privileged.

### Transformative Staff Experience
We believe that the foundation of our success is grounded in the dedication and passion of our staff towards the mission and vision of the University. This additional pillar envisages a supportive, developmental environment and culture in a community in which every member of the University is enabled to achieve his/her full potential and support the key objectives of the University strategy. This pillar builds progressively on the University's values, and responds to the priorities of our University, as we seek to support our people in delivering the University's Strategy 2020-2025.

### About the Vice Chancellor

Dr. Vincent Ogutu is the Vice-Chancellor of Strathmore University. He self-identifies as a teacher and is a Lecturer in Organisational Behaviour, Leadership, Social Entrepreneurship and Innovation. He is passionate about inspiring students at all levels to realise their potential for personal growth and social impact. He has given a TEDx talk at IESE Business School, Barcelona urging people to commit to the cause that "has their name written on it." He also uses television and social media as a platform to challenge people in the areas of leadership, entrepreneurship, creativity, talent development, and social impact.

Prior to his vice-chancellor role at the University, Dr. Ogutu was the Deputy Vice-Chancellor (Planning & Development), where he was charged with oversight of the University's strategy, fundraising, alumni relations, development projects and community outreach. He has also been the Vice Dean for Executive Talent Development at Strathmore University Business School, and the Director of the regional academies in Uganda, Rwanda and Tanzania. He was the Founding Director of the MBA for Executives Programme at SBS and led the Programme in its first three years. Before working at SBS, he was the Deputy Principal of Strathmore School where he fell in love with the teaching vocation and discovered how much of a difference it can make in people's lives.

Dr. Ogutu has a Ph.D in Organisational Management from Rutgers University, an MSc in Financial Economics from the University of London and a BA in Economics from the University of Nairobi. He undertook his doctoral studies full time in the United States on a Fulbright Scholarship. His research is on the Psychology of meaningful work and meaningful lives. He uses this knowledge to help organisations create meaningful work environments, and to help individuals to discover their path in life. He also uses it to uplift demographics that usually find their lives to be devoid of purpose (like prisoners and terminally ill patients).

While in the US, Dr. Vincent also focused on social entrepreneurship and innovation. He was one of seven moonshot pioneers chosen to present his tech idea on teleportation simulation at Google in New York in a Google NJ Solve for X competition. An accidental by-product of that Google adventure was the creation of his own step-by-step system for innovation that allows ordinary people to think outside the box and innovate. He dubbed it "The Innovation Algorithm". He has been invited to teach it in Kenya, Uganda, the US, Germany and Uruguay. Dr. Ogutu led Deloitte USA's cybersecurity team on a discussion of the psychological elements of security breaches. He was part of LaunchNJ, a team of professionals creating an ecosystem of entrepreneurs in NJ similar to the Silicon Valley dynamic.

He is now a champion of the Kenyan entrepreneurial ecosystem and has given keynote speeches at incubators and accelerators like iBizAfrica, the Nairobi Garage, Transformational Business Network and Amani Institute. He has given design-thinking workshops for organisations and networks of innovators like Strathmore Energy and Resource Center and Mirazi. He has also been a judge at entrepreneurial competitions organised by the Somo Project, and also the Women in Tech program of Standard Chartered Bank and iBizAfrica.

He currently sits on the boards of Strathmore University Foundation, B Lab East Africa (certifying companies for their social impact), Nafisika Trust (teaching entrepreneurial skills to prisoners), and is an advisor to several start-ups in Kenya and the US.

Dr. Vincent is passionate about getting businesses and individuals to use creativity and social innovation as a force for good.

### Vice Chancellor's New Year Message

Happy New Year everyone, and best wishes for 2025! I came back to the office this morning and was thrilled to meet so many of you who just wanted to say hello. What a fine way to begin experiencing Strathmore University's theme for 2025, "Personalised Attention."

This year's theme is a call to create an environment where every individual feels valued, supported, and empowered. Rooted in a human-centered approach, this theme reflects our commitment to providing customized service and care to all members of our community. At the core of this value, is the idea our Founder, St. Josemaria, had – that each person is unique, deserving of dignity, respect, and opportunities to flourish. When once asked whom he valued most at a university – whether a professor or a cleaner – he replied that he didn't actually know. He said it would ultimately depend on who put more love in his or her work.

In today's fast-paced world, where utilitarian mindsets often prevail, we are frequently conditioned to view others through the lens of what they can offer us – often defined by the question, "What's in it for me?" The essence of personalised attention directly challenges this mentality, urging us to recognise the inherent value in every person. Whether the person we're serving can reciprocate or not won't matter to us. We would be pleased to serve them well all the same.

As we embrace this theme, our focus is on cultivating a culture of excellence characterised by intentionality, attention to detail, and empathy. A lecturer, for example, cannot view students merely as names on a register, but as individuals capable of thriving under his or her guidance. Listening to students' feedback, incorporating their insights, and adapting our teaching and services are integral to creating a learning environment that helps them attain their fullest potential.

For our students, personalised attention is not only about receiving care – it also involves active engagement on your part. You are definitely more digital savvy than the rest of us, but I challenge you to regularly step away from the digital noise and invest time in meaningful interactions with your peers, faculty, and the broader university community. A friend of mine recently challenged me to take a digital fast one day per week – we'll see about that! Research has shown, however, that bonds formed during university years often evolve into life-long relationships that continue to impact one's personal and professional life. So reach out to someone, give them your full attention, then watch what happens.

I encourage each department (and each individual) to reflect on how they can bring this theme to life in their work – whether through their engagement with parents, suppliers, sponsors, donors, or any of our collaborators. If we fully embrace personalised attention, Strathmore will become that one place we all look forward to coming to. You will not be surprised to get a quick reply to a query, and you will be welcomed with a smile when you go to make a request. As the one serving others, you will not dread having problems presented to you. And you will also enjoy the fact that people don't take you for granted and they actually come back to say thank you!

Let us all strive to live personalised attention, #BecauseWeCare. Together, we can make 2025 a year where each individual is truly seen, heard, and empowered to thrive.

Dr. Vincent Ogutu,
Vice-Chancellor,
Strathmore University.

## FAQs

### Professional Branding Guidelines

1. **What is a professional branding guidelines policy?**
   A professional branding guidelines policy is a document that outlines the appropriate professional branding guidelines for an institution's internal stakeholders. Professional branding guidelines vary from company to company and are dependent on a company's culture and industry type.

2. **Why does Strathmore University have a professional branding guidelines policy?**
   The professional branding guidelines are an integral part of Strathmore's promise of holistic transformation of the student. At Strathmore, we prepare our students for the industry and this entails the aspect of how to portray oneself in the workplace through dress. It is therefore important that students embrace the professional branding guidelines as part of their day-to-day living as this will make their transition to the workplace smooth.

3. **What is the role of the fashion assistants?**
   The Fashion assistants at the University assist with the implementation of the University professional branding guidelines. They are mandated with guiding students who may miss the mark with regard to adherence to the policy.

4. **Where can I find the Strathmore University Professional branding guidelines policy?**
   The Strathmore University Professional branding guidelines has been detailed in the Student's handbook. The Professional branding guidelines can be found on pages 35-37. Here is the link to the Students' handbook: https://orientation.strathmore.edu/wpcontent/uploads/2020/04/Final-2020-Handbook.pdf

5. **Does the professional branding guidelines policy apply to staff members?**
   Yes, The Strathmore Professional branding guidelines apply to Staff and students.

6. **Does the professional branding guidelines apply to guests?**
   Yes, it does. Guests contribute to the learning environment for our students and are integral to the all-around formation of our students. Prior to events held within the University campus, participating organisations and attendees are furnished with the stipulated fashion guidelines.

7. **Who can I speak to in case I have a professional branding matter to raise?**
   The office in charge of the implementation of the professional branding guidelines policy is the Mentoring Services Department. You can reach them via email at Studentmentoring@strathmore.edu or call 0703034068.

### Strathmore Model Nomination Form
This form allows individuals to nominate themselves or others to become a Strathmore model for university media.
- Fields include: First Name, Middle Name, Last Name, Phone Number, Email Address, School/Faculty, Upload your selfie, Date of the shoot, Select category (Video/Photo)

### Strathmore Media Permit Form
This form is for external media organizations wishing to conduct shoots on campus.
- Fields include: First Name, Middle Name, Last Name, Phone Number, Email Address, Department/Faculty, Media/Organization name, Purpose of the shoot, Upload your ID, Email of contact person in department, Phone number of contact person, List of equipment, Date of the shoot

### Video and Photography Request Form
This form is for internal departments requesting video and photography services.
- Fields include: Full Name, Department, Phone Number, Email Address, Event Name, Event Venue, Audience, Strategic pillar alignment, Event dates and times, Event Description, Audio/Video Coverage options

### Design Request Form
For requesting design work from the communications department.
- Fields include: Your Name, Email Address, CC Your Manager, Phone Number, Department, Project title and purpose, Project type (multiple options), Brief description of the event, Printing intention, File uploads, Target audience, Medium of communication

### VIP Notification Form
For notifying the university about VIP visitors.
- Fields include: Full Name, Department, Phone Number, Email Address, Name of the VIP, VIP's Biography, Origin organization, Position in the Organization, Group/delegation details, University inviter details, Visit date and time, Purpose of the visit, Proposed format for visit, Vice-Chancellor participation details, University host details

### Email Dissemination Request Form
For requesting mass email communications to the university community.
- Fields include: Your Name, Email Address, Manager's email address, Phone Number, Department, Email Subject, Recipients, Mailing list uploads, Email content, File uploads, Email signature details

## Graduation Policy

### NOTICE TO 2025 GRADUANDS APPLICATION FOR GRADUATION
Strathmore University 2025 Graduation Instructions The Academic Division wishes to notify all members of the class of 2025 about graduation details relating to the application for graduation, clearance and gown collection.

Apply for graduation online by completing the Graduation Application form in the Students' Academic Module and pay the appropriate convocation fee as detailed here:
- PhD: 15,000
- Masters: 8,600 (SBS has an extra charge that will be communicated)
- Undergraduate: 8,100
- Diploma: 6,000

Convocation fees are paid to the SU bank account as with other fees. The same amounts can be charged on any excess fee payments already paid. Applications should be made after the confirmation of records and payment of fees balances with effect from Friday, 30th May 2025.

### Online Exit Survey
As part of the University improvement process, all graduating students are required to fill in the exit survey. This is done before application for clearance. Kindly give feedback to help improve the Strathmore Experience.

### Clearance
Students intending to graduate are required to clear from the university. The online link to Strathmore University clearance is https://apps.strathmore.edu/clearance (The link will be open on Wednesday, 23rd April 2025)

- Departments will clear all applicants who will have applied for graduation by 23rd June 2025. Clearance starts at the school or faculty confirming all students' details. "Pending status" means the school is checking and will clear one in due course.
- The clearance system will auto-clear one from all departments except Finance which has to close your financial records.
- Graduands who would like the order of names to be changed on the graduation certificate are required to do so through their school administrators.
- University transcripts follow the order in AMS (Surname- First Name- Other Names)
- Collection of graduation gowns from schools is scheduled for Monday, 16th June 2025 and should be returned by Friday, 8th August 2025. A late gown return will attract a daily surcharge of KES 200.
- You will be expected to surrender your SU ID as you collect your gown or when picking up your certificate and transcripts.

Students intending to graduate in 2025 should note the academic regulations indicate that:
- A student who has not satisfied all the requirements for graduation will not be awarded the corresponding diploma/conferred with the corresponding degree.
- Students with a graduation grievance have to withdraw from the graduation process until the matter is resolved.
- Once the diploma is awarded/degree is conferred during the graduation ceremony, no changes will be made.
- For the planning and organisation of the graduation ceremony; only those students who have filed an Application for Graduation by the stipulated time will be included in the graduation list.

For any clarifications and concerns, kindly write to: registraracademic@strathmore.edu or contact your School Administrator.

The Academic Division congratulates the class of 2025!

Stephen Ng'ang'a
University Registrar
