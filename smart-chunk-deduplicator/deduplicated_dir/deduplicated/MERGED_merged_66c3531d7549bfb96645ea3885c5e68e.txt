corporate apostolic undertaking of Opus Dei, inspired by St. Josemaria Escriva. Opus Dei is a Personal Prelature of the Catholic Church erected by Pope St. John Paul II on 28th November 1982. Through its activities, it seeks to spread the message that work, study, sport family life, and other ordinary activities are occasions for spiritual union with Jesus Christ.

--- Additional Information ---
The spiritual guidance is entrusted to Opus Dei, a Prelature of the Catholic Church. Opus Dei was founded in 1928 by Saint Josemara Escriv. He used to teach the message that everyone can reach sanctity, human and Christian perfection in their ordinary work. In this context, care for detail, work well done, service to others, respect for the dignity of persons, and ethical conduct, are crucial as shown in the day-to-day life of Strathmore.

--- Additional Information ---
Corporate apostolic initiatives promoted by Opus Dei, such as Strathmore University, are characterized by the presence of some faithful of Opus Dei, who together with ordinary citizens, undertake secular, lay activities, in accordance with the law of the land, based on professional work, and always imbued with a Christian spirit.
