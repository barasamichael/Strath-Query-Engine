Special retake Examination** A candidate who for serious reasons such as medical or compassionate grounds cannot sit a given retake examination when offered shall retake the unit when next offered. The rules governing retake examinations apply. **6. Special Ordinary Examination** Special Ordinary Examinations shall normally be offered on medical grounds and compassionate grounds only.

--- Additional Information ---
**Compassionate Grounds** A candidate who is prevented by circumstances beyond his or her control from attending an ordinary examination may apply for a special ordinary examination on compassionate grounds. A candidate who fails in any special ordinary examination shall retake the examination in the failed unit(s) when next offered.

--- Additional Information ---
**Medical Grounds** - A candidate who is prevented by illness from attending an examination, or a candidate who believes that illness has significantly affected his or her preparation for or performance at an examination, may apply for a special ordinary examination on medical grounds.

--- Additional Information ---
Candidates should note the following: - Each application for a special ordinary examination on medical or compassionate grounds is considered on its merits and making an application does not guarantee that a special ordinary examination will be granted. - Given that such special ordinary examinations are not automatically offered, candidates are encouraged to sit the ordinary University examination.
