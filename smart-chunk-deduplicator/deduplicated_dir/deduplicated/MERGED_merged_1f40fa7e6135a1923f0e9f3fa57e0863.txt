This field course is developed to advance students knowledge and understanding of conservation practice including wildlife behaviour and ecology, wildlife management in the wild, land use change and habitat quality, wildlife adaptation and social behaviour, human interactions with wildlife, conservation and livelihoods, socio-economic, legal implications of conflicts at conservation areas, business of conservation, conservation entrepreneurship and survival skills in the field among other

--- Additional Information ---
elements. The course is developed for students who have a special interest in experiencing conservation in action, wildlife behaviour and human-wildlife interactions in the natural environment. It targets students who are keen to engage in experiential learning and teamwork and research-based approach in an active multi-cultural field setting.
