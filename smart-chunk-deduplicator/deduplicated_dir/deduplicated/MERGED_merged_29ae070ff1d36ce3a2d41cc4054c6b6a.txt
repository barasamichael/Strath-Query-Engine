

--- MERGED CHUNK (from 3 sources) ---

The first undergraduate students to enroll in these faculties completed their 4-year degree course in December 2004 and graduated in August 2005. In June 2007, Kenya's Commission for Higher Education approved the award of a charter to Strathmore University. The University's Charter was gazetted under the Universities Act (CAP 210B) in the 'Kenya Gazette' supplement no. 47 (legislative supplement no. 27) via legal notice no. 86.

--- Additional Information ---
- **2004**: The first undergraduate students enrolled in these faculties completed their 4-year degree course in December. - **2005**: Establishment of Strathmore Business School. - **2008**: The University Charter was gazetted on June 23rd, under the 'Kenya Gazette' supplement no. 47. - **2014**: Completion of the Sir Thomas More Building.

--- Additional Information ---
- **2005**: Strathmore launches Strathmore Business School (SBS), as a graduate school to fill in the existing gap in leadership & management in Executive Education and MBA. - **2007**: Strathmore is awarded a Charter by the Government of Kenya giving it full legal recognition to operate as a University. - **2009**: Strathmore launches the School of Finance & Applied Economics (SFAE) to expand the programme offering from the initial focus on business & information technology.
